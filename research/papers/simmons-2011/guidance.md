# Guidance — simmons-2011 (paper)

> Targeted questions for `/summarize-source`. Attention-directing, not leading. Maintained by us.
> Companion source for [[garden-of-forking-paths]] — capture "researcher degrees of freedom," the
> quantified false-positive inflation, and the disclosure remedy. Numbers are load-bearing.

## Must capture
- The exact definition/coining of "**researcher degrees of freedom**" — what flexibilities count
  (data collection stopping rules, choice of conditions/covariates, exclusions, outcome measures,
  reporting subsets).
- **The quantified result:** by how much does exploiting these flexibilities inflate the
  false-positive rate above nominal 0.05? Capture the exact simulation numbers (per-flexibility and
  combined; the headline "≈61%"-style figure and the conditions producing it) — pin them precisely.
- The two actual experiments demonstrating "anything can be significant" (the impossible-result demo,
  e.g. the age / music manipulation) — capture what was shown.
- **The remedy — the disclosure requirements ("21-word solution" / the 6 requirements for authors +
  4 guidelines for reviewers):** capture the specific list verbatim as functional strings (they are a
  checklist, facts not prose).
- How the paper frames the mechanism vs. deliberate fraud (undisclosed flexibility, not fabrication).

## Must-quote (own-words if license restrictive — SAGE/Psych Science; functional strings verbatim)
- The definition of researcher degrees of freedom.
- The headline false-positive-rate figure(s) and the conditions.
- The author-requirements list (the "21-word solution").

## Access / version notes
- Simmons JP, Nelson LD, Simonsohn U. "False-Positive Psychology: Undisclosed Flexibility in Data
  Collection and Analysis Allows Presenting Anything as Significant." *Psychological Science*
  22(11):1359–1366, 2011. DOI 10.1177/0956797611417632. SAGE (likely paywalled); open copies: SSRN
  abstract_id=1850704, ResearchGate, the authors' hosts. Data at Journal of Open Psychology Data
  (10.5334/jopd.aa).
- If only the abstract/paywalled version is reachable, say so and capture what you can; the numeric
  figures + the requirements list are the recovery targets — get them from an openly readable copy
  (SSRN preprint) if the publisher text is blocked, and flag which version you read.
