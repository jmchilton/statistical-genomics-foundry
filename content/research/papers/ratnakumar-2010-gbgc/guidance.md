# Guidance — ratnakumar-2010-gbgc (paper, gBGC surrogate)

> Phase-5 surrogate for the gBGC gap. Our primary gBGC note (`galtier-duret-2007-gbgc`) is
> abstract-only; this free-to-read Royal Society review carries the W→S mechanism and the
> operational discrimination criteria the abstract left in the paywalled body.

License: © 2010 The Royal Society, free-to-read on PMC, **no CC license → own-words mode**;
keep W→S / S→W substitution-class labels and any numeric values verbatim as functional strings.
Access: PMC2935097 full text (https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC2935097/). Fig. 2 is an
image — read directionality from its caption, not the plotted points.

## Must capture (attention-directing — do NOT "confirm X"; ask "does the source state X? quote it")
- How does the source describe the **MECHANISM** by which GC-biased gene conversion preferentially
  fixes Weak→Strong (A/T→G/C) alleles during meiotic recombination, and how it elevates the
  substitution rate at functional sites without positive selection? Quote the mechanism sentences.
- What **OPERATIONAL CRITERIA** does the source give to distinguish a gBGC-driven acceleration from a
  selection-driven one? Enumerate each verbatim (directionality W→S vs S→W; effect on flanking
  neutral sites; association with male/high recombination; GC-content / equilibrium-GC signals).
- Does the source state **ANY numeric threshold** (e.g. a W→S : S→W ratio, a recombination-rate
  cutoff)? If yes, quote it exactly; if no, record "no numeric threshold stated" (this is expected —
  the "W→S ratio > 1.5" figure is a convention, not this paper's number).
- What **magnitude** of elevated-dN/dS cases does the paper attribute to gBGC rather than selection?
  Capture the exact percentages and the lineage/branch conditions, and **label them as the paper's
  own re-analysis** (not a general field number).
- Does the source say gBGC should be treated as an **extended NULL to exclude before inferring
  adaptation**? Quote the framing.
