## 1. Lens & scope
What the R/Bioconductor ecosystem already offers for **doing** statistical-genomics analysis well (Family A) and **refereeing** its statistical validity (Family B), plus the emerging LLM/agent layer over R/Bioconductor — assessed against our goal of a portable, inspectable "skills" KB that keeps an LLM both productive and statistically honest.

## 3. Closest prior art (top 3)
1. **POPPER (2502.09858)** [verified] — closest to our structural bet: an agent that does **not** self-certify but hands hypotheses to an external sequential-falsification gate with **provable Type-I error control**. The Family-B "doing-hands-off-to-referee" pattern, already implemented and biology-applicable. Differs: validates *claims/hypotheses*, not *method choices*; isn't a portable inspectable skills KB.
2. **countsplit / data-thinning** [verified] — canonical worked example of the whole thesis at the method level: a *named* invalidity (double-dipping / circular inference) paired with a concrete *empirical* remedy. Family B wants a "countsplit-style remedy library."
3. **DHARMa** [verified] — the model-assumption analog: converts "are the assumptions met?" into simulation-based, interpretable empirical diagnostics with explicit tests. Pattern to generalize across model families.

## 4. White space / gaps
- **No statistical-validity referee in the standard gates.** Bioconductor's review process — the field's flagship refereeing mechanism — explicitly checks *software*, not *method validity* [verified]. The "did you double-dip / confound / violate assumptions / inflate Type-I" review is left entirely to ad-hoc human peer review. This is the central gap Family B targets.
- **Diagnostics are scattered, not orchestrated.** DHARMa (assumptions), countsplit (double-dipping), simr (power), sva/RUVSeq (confounders), p-value-histogram conventions all exist as **individual** tools; nobody packages them into a **portable, inspectable checklist a referee agent runs** ("for analysis of type X, here are the empirical checks the field trusts").
- **The "invented method" failure is essentially unguarded.** Existing R tooling validates *known* methods' assumptions; nothing systematically detects an LLM *fabricating* a plausible-but-novel method. Closest defense is POPPER-style falsification of the *output claim*, not auditing the *method's derivation* — a genuine open niche.
- **"Doing" agents lack the referee handoff.** Biomni, CellAgent, BioMANIA, scExtract etc. optimize **productivity (wiring/scale/reproduction)** and explicitly lean on *human* oversight for correctness [verified for Biomni/BioMANIA] — exactly the productive-but-not-honest profile our project flags.
- **Negative-control / null-calibration as a first-class skill** is underdeveloped: control genes (RUVSeq), simulated doublets (scDblFinder), permutation nulls exist in pockets, but there's no unifying "construct the negative control / permutation null the field trusts here" abstraction.

## 5. Agentic / LLM-specific efforts (called out)
- **Family A doers:** Biomni [verified], CellAgent/CASSIA/scExtract/AnnoAgent (LLM4Cell survey) [secondary], BioMANIA [verified]. All productivity-first; correctness ≈ human-in-loop or heuristic.
- **Family B referees (rare):** **POPPER** [verified] stands out — empirical-falsification gate with Type-I control. No R/Bioconductor-native referee agent found.
- **Enabling infra:** **MCPmed** [verified] (agent-actionable tool layer with a "verification" aspiration).
- **Evidence base for the thesis:** "When Stability Fails" (stability ≠ correctness) [verified], BioAgentBench [secondary], statistical-test-selection eval showing LLMs mis-select tests [secondary]. These corroborate the "plausible-but-invalid, self-certified" failure mode.
- **Honest caveat:** found **no** project combining (a) an R/Bioconductor skills KB for *doing* with (b) a non-self-certifying statistical *referee* gate using the field's empirical checks. That combination appears to be white space [inference].

**One-line takeaway:** the R/Bioconductor world has a rich, mature *catalog of empirical validity checks* (countsplit, DHARMa, simr, sva/RUVSeq, p-value-histogram/dispersion diagnostics) and excellent *doing* curricula (OSCA), but its institutional referee gate (package review) and its new LLM agents (Biomni et al.) **both decline to referee statistical validity** — leaving our "non-self-certifying referee that runs the field's trusted empirical checks" as genuine white space, with POPPER the nearest agentic prior art.
