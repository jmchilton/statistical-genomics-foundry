# Guidance — hochberg-thornton-2017-resurrection (paper)

> Attention-directing. The review behind the protein-resurrection workflow, the epistasis failure
> mode, and the "test N alternative constructs at ambiguous sites; report a functional range, not the
> single ML sequence" operational rule.

## Must capture
- The epistasis problem for ASR: why the per-site-independent ML ancestral sequence can be an
  untested / never-existing combination of states, and how compensatory / coupled substitutions
  break the site-independence assumption. Capture the review's own statement.
- The operational strategy for handling reconstruction uncertainty: alternative-construct design at
  ambiguous positions, experimental testing, reporting a range. Capture exactly what the review
  recommends (thresholds it names, how many constructs, how ambiguity is defined) vs. what the skill
  adds. If the review names a posterior cutoff for "ambiguous," capture it; if not, that cutoff is
  convention (flag it).
- Any statement on whether reconstructed ancestors are systematically BIASED (e.g. toward stability/
  most-probable states) and how robust functional conclusions are to statistical uncertainty.
- Named strategies/tools for epistasis-aware or indel-aware reconstruction the review endorses.

## Must-quote (Annual Reviews ARR → own-words paraphrase only)
- The statement that the single ML ancestral sequence can be a non-existent state combination under
  epistasis.
- The recommendation to test multiple constructs / report a functional range.
