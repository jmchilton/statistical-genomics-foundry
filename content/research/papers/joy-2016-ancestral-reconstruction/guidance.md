# Guidance — joy-2016-ancestral-reconstruction (paper / review)

> Attention-directing. An OA (CC BY) review of ancestral reconstruction — ingested as the accessible
> surrogate carrying the method taxonomy + validity cautions that the paywalled Cunningham 1999 note
> could not supply (single-point-estimate inadequacy; parsimony's equal-rates assumption). Broad
> review: capture the method families and their stated limitations, not every example.

## Must capture
- The **method taxonomy** as the review frames it: maximum parsimony (Fitch), maximum likelihood
  (marginal reconstruction), and Bayesian ASR — what each computes and the assumptions each makes.
- **Parsimony's equal-rates assumption**: capture the review's own statement that parsimony (Fitch)
  treats all state changes as equally likely, and why that is "often unrealistic." Quote it.
- **Single point estimate is inadequate under a multimodal likelihood**: the review's statement that
  when the likelihood surface is nonconvex / has multiple optima, a single point estimate cannot
  adequately represent the reconstruction. Quote it — this is the load-bearing uncertainty caution.
- Any statement on **asymmetric transition rates** and their effect on reconstruction (direction of
  bias, which state gets favoured). If the review states a direction, capture it verbatim; if it does
  NOT, say so — the direction is contested elsewhere and we do not want a laundered gloss.
- How the review recommends **reporting/handling uncertainty** (marginal posteriors, model choice,
  not over-interpreting a single reconstruction), if stated.
- Any named **software/tools** the review points to.

## Must-quote (PLoS Comput Biol = CC BY → short verbatim load-bearing quotes allowed, with section
   location)
- The parsimony equal-rates-assumption sentence.
- The single-point-estimate-inadequate sentence.

  Biol 12(7):e1004763. DOI 10.1371/journal.pcbi.1004763. Fully OA (CC BY). URL:
  https://journals.plos.org/ploscompbiol/article?id=10.1371/journal.pcbi.1004763 . This is a *review*
  surrogate — attribute facts to Joy et al. 2016, do not launder them into a citation of Cunningham 1999
