# Multivariate Methods for Heterogeneous Data — MSMB Chapter 9 (summary)

_Own-words summary of an external source (see frontmatter for license/attribution). Not a substitute for the original chapter._

## 1. Scope

This chapter extends linear dimensionality reduction to *heterogeneous* and *non-coordinate* data: settings where observations are described not by a clean matrix of continuous variables but by pairwise distances or (dis)similarities, by categorical/contingency-table counts, by a mix of continuous and categorical variables plus contextual covariates, or by *several* data tables measured on the same samples. It equips a reader to build low-dimensional "maps" (ordinations) from distance matrices, to expose and visualize dependencies in categorical data, to detect hidden one-dimensional structure (gradients, orderings, "pseudotime"), to recognize and remove batch/nuisance effects, and to measure and exploit association *between* whole tables from different data domains. The treatment is deliberately applied and example-driven; it states methods and their behavior but often omits derivations.

## 2. Concepts & Methods

- **Multidimensional scaling (MDS) / Principal Coordinates Analysis (PCoA).** Input: an $n\times n$ matrix of pairwise distances (or squared distances), *not* a feature matrix. Output: coordinates of points in a low-dimensional Euclidean space whose interpoint distances approximate the given ones, plus eigenvalues for a screeplot. Mechanism: form the squared-distance matrix (the chapter writes the elementwise product $D\bullet D$, distinct from matrix products $DD$ or $D^2$); double-center it with the centering matrix $H = I - \tfrac{1}{n}\mathbf{1}\mathbf{1}^t$ and scale by $-\tfrac12$ to recover a cross-product (Gram) matrix $B = -\tfrac12\, H (D\bullet D)\, H$; eigendecompose $B = U\Lambda U^t$; take coordinates $\tilde X = U\Lambda^{1/2}$, keeping the leading $r$ axes. This is the distance-space analogue of PCA's best rank-$r$ approximation via SVD. Relation to PCA: when distances are Euclidean from an actual feature matrix, MDS reproduces PCA's configuration; but MDS has only *one* mode (the sample points) — there is no dual variable space, hence **no loadings and no biplot**, which limits interpretation.
- **Non-metric MDS (NMDS).** A rank-based, robust variant. Instead of matching distance *values*, it seeks a monotone transformation $f$ of the input dissimilarities $d$ and a low-dimensional configuration with distances $\tilde d$ so that $f(d)\approx\tilde d$ — i.e., only the *ordering* of distances must be preserved. Fit quality is the standardized residual sum of squares, the **stress**: $\text{stress}^2 = \frac{\sum (f(d)-\tilde d)^2}{\sum d^2}$. Unlike MDS/PCA it is non-sequential: the target dimension $k$ must be fixed in advance, the optimization is iterative and randomly initialized, and there is no "percent variance per axis."
- **Robustness (general principle).** A method is robust if a few outliers cannot dominate it. Quantified by the **breakdown point** — the fraction of data that must be corrupted to move an estimate arbitrarily: $1/2$ for the median, only $1/n$ for the mean. Rank-based and nonparametric reductions inherit this robustness; NMDS is the ordination instance.
- **Supplementary information / supplementary points.** Categorical covariates (batch, day, protocol, treatment, location, time) are folded into a continuous ordination by projecting the *group mean* (barycenter) of points sharing a level as an extra marker, *without* including those points in the matrix decomposition. This lets a few categorical variables annotate a PCA/MDS map and exposes nuisance structure such as **batch effects**.
- **Correspondence Analysis (CA) / dual scaling.** Input: a two-way contingency table $\mathbf N$ of counts ($I$ rows, $J$ columns, grand total $n_{\cdot\cdot}$). It decomposes the *departure from independence* the way PCA decomposes variance. Under independence each cell would be $n_{ij}=\frac{n_{i\cdot}}{n}\frac{n_{\cdot j}}{n}\,n$ (outer product of row and column marginal proportions); the total departure is the $\chi^2$ statistic $\mathcal X^2=\sum_{i,j}\frac{(n_{ij}-\text{expected})^2}{\text{expected}}$, and CA is a *weighted* SVD/MDS using the **$\chi^2$ distance** in place of variance. Output: a symmetric **biplot** where rows and columns share status and are co-plotted; it has a **barycentric property** — each row point sits at the weighted center of gravity of the column points (weights = its conditional counts), so spatial proximity of a row level and a column level encodes strong co-occurrence. CA is the natural follow-up to a significant $\chi^2$ test, turning "not independent" into "*which* levels associate, positively or negatively."
- **Ordination as latent-variable discovery.** Just as clustering recovers a hidden *categorical* factor, ordination recovers a hidden *continuous* ordering or gradient. A **horseshoe / arch** in the first two coordinates is a signature of an underlying sequential latent variable (a gradient or chronology). Historically used for ecological gradients and **seriation** (ordering objects by presence/absence patterns, e.g. dating artifacts); modern analogue is recovering developmental trajectories / pseudotime in single-cell data.
- **Local / nonlinear embedding methods.** When only *local* distances are trustworthy, downweight large distances. A radial-basis kernel $1-\exp\!\big(-\frac{d(x,y)^2}{\sigma^2}\big)$ saturates toward 1 for large $d$, discounting far-apart pairs. **t-SNE** generalizes this by letting $\sigma^2$ vary locally (with a normalization), converting high-dimensional proximities into a probability distribution over point pairs and reconstructing a low-dimensional layout that reproduces those probabilities. Related families named: **isomap**, **local linear embedding (LLE)**, **Laplacian eigenmaps**, **diffusion maps** (a Markov/affinity-kernel approach using local probabilistic transitions, then eigendecomposition), **local principal curves**, and **principal curves** (nonlinear analogue of a principal axis). Software named: `Rtsne`, `RDRToolbox`, `diffusionMap`, `kernlab`, `LPCM`, `princurve`.
- **Multitable / co-variation measures.** Generalize variance to **inertia** (a weighted sum of squared distances; the $\chi^2$ statistic is one instance, phylogenetic diversity another). To compare *two tables* on the same samples:
  - **Mantel coefficient** — correlate the two distance/dissimilarity matrices $D^X, D^Y$ after vectorizing their off-diagonal entries: $r_m = \frac{\sum_{i\neq j}(d^X_{ij}-\bar d^X)(d^Y_{ij}-\bar d^Y)}{\sqrt{\sum_{i\neq j}(d^X_{ij}-\bar d^X)^2}\;\sqrt{\sum_{i\neq j}(d^Y_{ij}-\bar d^Y)^2}}$. Distinguished from RV/dCov by the *absence of double centering*.
  - **RV coefficient** — a correlation-like similarity between two whole tables $A,B$, $RV(A,B)=\frac{\mathrm{Tr}(A'BAB')}{\sqrt{\mathrm{Tr}(A'A)}\sqrt{\mathrm{Tr}(B'B)}}\in[0,1]$.
  - **Canonical Correlation Analysis (CCA, Hotelling).** Find linear combinations $U=Xa$, $V=Yb$ of two variable sets maximizing $\mathrm{cor}(U,V)$ subject to unit variance; the maximizing $\rho_1=\max_{a,b}\mathrm{cor}(Xa,Yb)$ is the first canonical correlation, with higher orthogonal canonical variates $\rho_1\ge\rho_2\ge\cdots$ found stepwise. A PCA-like method whose maximized quantity is cross-table covariance.
  - **Sparse CCA (sCCA).** CCA with an $L_1$ penalty (`penaltyx`, `penaltyz`) forcing most coefficients to zero, used as a *feature-screening* step in the high-dimensional regime (many more features than samples); selected features then feed an ordinary PCA (a **triplot**: samples, multiple feature domains, and axes together).
  - **Constrained Correspondence Analysis (CCpnA).** CA constrained so the recovered axes lie in the span of supplied *explanatory* (environmental) variables — a correspondence-analysis analogue of constrained regression. Positions reflect both species/feature signatures *and* environmental covariates, unlike plain CA/PCA. The chapter deliberately renames it CCpnA to avoid colliding with "CCA" (Canonical Correlation), warning that several R packages overload the name `cca`.
- **Named software:** `cmdscale` (classical MDS), `metaMDS` (NMDS), `dudi.coa`/`ade4`, `CA`/`FactoMineR`, `CCA`/`ordinate`/`phyloseq`, `vegan`, `PMA` (sCCA), `ComBat`/`sva`, `MatrixCorrelation`, `ecodist`; Bioconductor `SummarizedExperiment` for keeping assay + covariate data aligned.

## 3. When It Applies

- **MDS/PCoA:** when objects have meaningful pairwise distances/dissimilarities but *no natural coordinates* (trees, sequences, images, documents), or when you want a PCA-style map directly from a distance matrix. Use the screeplot's drop to choose dimensionality.
- **NMDS:** when the *scale* of the dissimilarities is uncertain or untrustworthy, only their ordering is meaningful, or outliers/large distances would distort a least-squares fit. For the worked color-confusion data, metric and non-metric solutions nearly coincided — the robust version costs little and protects against scale misspecification.
- **Supplementary points:** when most variables are continuous but a few categorical covariates (batch, treatment) must be overlaid to *diagnose* or *adjust for* their influence without letting them drive the decomposition.
- **Correspondence Analysis:** for two (or, via MCA, more) categorical variables / contingency tables, *after* a significant $\chi^2$ test, to locate and interpret which level-pairs drive the dependence. Rows and columns have symmetric status (no response/predictor split).
- **Local/nonlinear methods (t-SNE, diffusion maps, isomap, LLE):** when structure is a low-dimensional *manifold* or *gradient* and only local distances are reliable; preferred over global MDS when faraway distances are noisy or the manifold is curved.
- **Multitable methods:** when several assays are measured on the same samples. Choose by goal — **Mantel** to test whether two *distance* structures are related; **RV** for a single scalar table-similarity; **CCA/sCCA** to extract the *correlated linear signal* between two variable sets (sCCA when features ≫ samples); **CCpnA** when you want ordination guided by, but not strictly supervised on, several environmental covariates. CCpnA is "less aggressive than supervised methods" and can use several covariates at once; plain CA has *more power* to detect association when environmental effects are weak.

## 4. Assumptions & Validity Conditions

- **MDS:** the input should be (approximately) a distance/dissimilarity matrix. Exact Euclidean reproduction in low dimensions is generally impossible (e.g., curved-surface data into a plane); one accepts an approximate embedding. **Negative eigenvalues can appear** in `cmdscale` (unlike PCA) — a normal consequence of non-Euclidean distances, not an error.
- **Orientation is arbitrary.** Because distances are invariant to rotation and reflection, the recovered map is determined only up to rotation/axis-flip; sign and orientation carry *no meaning* and can vary with trivial data or platform changes.
- **NMDS:** target dimension $k$ must be fixed before fitting; the fit is a stochastic optimization, so results depend on initialization and should be replicated.
- **CA / $\chi^2$:** rests on the independence (outer-product) model of expected counts; the $\chi^2$ approximation degrades when expected cell counts are very small. Rows and columns are treated symmetrically (no supervised structure).
- **Classical CCA:** assumes $p\le n$ and $q\le n$ with $X,Y$ of full column rank; columns standardized to mean 0, variance 1. These fail in the high-dimensional ($p,q\gg n$) regime, which is exactly why sCCA's penalty is needed.
- **Mantel:** the entries of a distance matrix are *not independent* (each object appears in many pairs), so the usual sampling theory for a correlation coefficient does **not** apply; significance must be obtained by permutation.
- **Robust methods (NMDS, rank transforms, kernelized/local methods):** appropriate precisely when the load-bearing concern is outliers or unreliable large distances; their value is conditional on that being the actual data problem.

## 5. Failure Modes & Invalidity Patterns

- **Confounding of effects.** If two nuisance factors change together (e.g., protocol *and* array both differ between days), an ordination shows a clean separation but you *cannot attribute* it to either cause. Warning sign: groups separate along an axis that aligns with a batch/date variable. Consequence: the apparent effect is uninterpretable. Detection/fix shown explicitly: collect an additional deconfounding batch that breaks the lockstep (one factor held at level A while the other moves to level B); if the new group overlaps the group sharing its true causal level, that factor is the driver. The chapter stresses analyzing data *as collected* so design flaws are caught in time, not "post mortem."
- **Treating covariates as "metadata."** Storing batch/clinical/time information separately from assay data (or encoding a factor as a number that looks continuous) invites lost linkages and category/scale confusion, making later analyses impossible or misleading. The chapter explicitly discourages the "metadata" framing.
- **Over-interpreting MDS maps.** No biplot/loadings exist, so axes have no variable-level meaning; interpretation must come from inspecting extreme points and their contrasts, not from reading axes as in PCA.
- **Reading orientation/sign as signal.** Flipped axes between two analyses (e.g., the sign inversion on a second axis when adding a batch) have *no biological relevance*; mistaking them for a finding is an error.
- **t-SNE artifacts.** t-SNE is *not robust* and **artificially separates clusters**; inter-cluster distances and apparent gaps are *not* interpretable on a common scale and depend on local point density and on the perplexity/$\sigma$ tuning. Treating t-SNE separations as true distances, or cluster sizes/gaps as meaningful, is invalid. (The chapter points to a resource on how easily t-SNE is misread.)
- **Spurious co-variation in high dimensions.** With many variables per table, *any* two highly correlated linear combinations can be found by chance — too many degrees of freedom. A near-perfect canonical/sCCA correlation (e.g., 0.99) does *not* certify a real shared signal: the scores may be far from bivariate-normal, and *other, equally good* feature subsets may exist. sCCA minimizes redundancy but offers **no guarantee** the selected features are the "true" ones. Be wary of the headline correlation.
- **Mantel mis-significance.** Using ordinary correlation p-values for a Mantel coefficient ignores within-matrix dependence and gives wrong significance; the chapter also flags that the Mantel test itself has documented criticisms.
- **Outlier domination in metric MDS.** A single point with large distances to everything can skew the entire configuration; this is the motivation for rank-based/robust and local approaches.

## 6. Empirical Checks & Calibration

- **Screeplot of eigenvalues** (MDS/CA): a pronounced drop after the first few eigenvalues indicates the effective dimensionality (e.g., a clear break after two axes ⇒ a 2-D embedding suffices; a three-eigenvalue plateau ⇒ genuinely 3-D structure). For CA, the eigenvalue magnitudes indicate how much $\chi^2$/inertia each axis captures.
- **Stress curve / "simili-screeplot" for NMDS:** rerun the optimization across $k=1,2,3,\dots$ and watch where stress drops steeply; take $k$ after the steep drop. Because each fit is random, run *many replicates per $k$* (e.g., on the order of 100) and inspect **boxplots of stress** to judge both the level and the *stability* of the solution.
- **Shepard plot:** scatter original dissimilarities against reconstructed distances; a tight monotone relationship signals a faithful embedding for that $k$.
- **Cross-method agreement:** compare metric vs. non-metric MDS configurations, and MDS under different distances (e.g., $\ell_1$ vs. $L_2$) — near-identical maps and near-identical eigenvalue profiles are reassurance that the recovered structure is real and not an artifact of one metric; large disagreement flags fragility.
- **Sensitivity to tuning:** vary the kernel $\sigma$ (and t-SNE perplexity/dimension) and check whether structure persists; structure that appears only at one setting is suspect.
- **Permutation testing for table association:** the Mantel coefficient's null distribution is obtained by permuting one matrix's labels and recomputing the statistic (the only valid significance route given dependent distances); `RV.test` similarly assesses table-level association before committing to a multitable model.
- **Ground-truth / known-structure checks:** validating an embedding against a known configuration when one exists (the geography example reconstructs true relative city positions up to rotation; ordering recovered by seriation can be checked against known endpoints) demonstrates the method recovers truth and exposes its rotation/reflection ambiguity.
- **Batch-removal verification:** after adjusting (group-mean recentering, or `ComBat`/empirical-Bayes), redo the ordination and confirm the previously separated batch groups now overlap near the origin while the biological clustering remains.
- **Rank/threshold transforms as robustness controls:** replacing values by ranks, and tying low (noise-level) values at a threshold, prevents meaningless large rank jumps among absent/low-abundance features from creating spurious signal.

## 7. Interpretation, Guidance & Trade-offs

- **What a map does and does not license.** Proximity in an MDS/NMDS map means similar (small distance); axes have no intrinsic meaning, and sign/orientation are arbitrary. In CA, proximity of a row level and a column level means strong co-occurrence (barycentric reading), and the biplot shows *positive vs. negative* departures from independence (e.g., one phenotype pairing occurs more than expected, another less). t-SNE clusters are real groupings, but *between-cluster distances and gap sizes are not quantitatively meaningful*.
- **CA as the honest sequel to $\chi^2$.** A significant $\chi^2$ only says "dependent"; CA localizes the dependence to specific level-pairs and quantifies what fraction of the total $\chi^2$/inertia the first axes explain — report that percentage as the analogue of "variance explained."
- **Robust vs. exact trade-off.** Metric MDS uses distance values (more information, less robust); NMDS uses only ranks (robust to outliers and scale misspecification, but loses the metric and the per-axis variance accounting). When both agree, prefer the simpler interpretation; when they disagree, trust the robust one if outliers are plausible.
- **Global vs. local trade-off.** Global methods (MDS) faithfully represent large distances but can be dominated by them; local/kernel methods (t-SNE, diffusion maps, isomap) sacrifice global geometry to render local/manifold structure and gradients — choose by whether the scientific target is overall configuration or a trajectory.
- **Keep data and covariates together.** Treat batch/time/protocol/clinical covariates as first-class data tightly linked to the assays (e.g., in a `SummarizedExperiment`); this both enables correct filtering and makes batch effects detectable as supplementary points.
- **Report for trustworthiness:** the distance/dissimilarity used, the chosen dimensionality and how it was selected (scree/stress), replicate stability for stochastic methods, tuning parameters (kernel $\sigma$, perplexity, sparsity penalties) and the number of features selected, and any batch-adjustment applied. For sCCA, hold in mind and disclose that the selected feature set is one of possibly many and the reported canonical correlation is optimistic.
- **Sparse/regularized defaults:** in the high-dimensional regime prefer the regularized ($L_1$-penalized) CCA over classical CCA, and tune penalties toward *more* sparsity than defaults to keep the result interpretable.

## 8. Connections & Key Terms

**Builds on:** PCA and SVD as best low-rank approximation (the prior continuous-only ordination); clustering as discovery of a hidden *categorical* latent factor; the $\chi^2$ test of independence for contingency tables; correlation/covariance for paired continuous variables; rank-based/nonparametric robustness. **Sets up:** trajectory/pseudotime and developmental-lineage analysis via gradients and manifolds; networks and trees (graph/tree distances as ordination input, phylogenetic diversity as inertia); multi-omics data integration via multitable correlation; multiple correspondence analysis (MCA) for many categorical (and binned continuous) variables; regularization/overfitting concerns in high-dimensional supervised learning.

**Key terms:**
- **Ordination** — building a low-dimensional map that reveals a hidden continuous ordering/gradient (the continuous analogue of clustering).
- **MDS / PCoA** — recovering point coordinates from a distance matrix by double-centering and eigendecomposition; PCA's distance-space cousin.
- **NMDS** — rank-only, robust MDS minimizing *stress*.
- **Stress** — standardized residual sum of squares between input dissimilarities (monotonically transformed) and map distances.
- **Breakdown point** — fraction of data corruption an estimator tolerates before failing ($1/2$ median, $1/n$ mean); a robustness measure.
- **Centering matrix $H$** — $I-\tfrac1n\mathbf{1}\mathbf{1}^t$; subtracts column/row means to remove origin dependence.
- **Horseshoe / arch** — curved point pattern signaling an underlying one-dimensional gradient or chronology; basis of seriation.
- **Seriation** — ordering objects along a latent sequence from presence/absence or abundance patterns.
- **Contingency table** — cross-tabulated co-occurrence counts of two categorical variables.
- **$\chi^2$ distance / statistic** — weighted measure of a table's departure from the independence (marginal outer-product) model; CA's analogue of variance.
- **Correspondence Analysis (CA)** — weighted SVD/MDS of $\chi^2$ distances yielding a symmetric biplot of row and column levels; barycentric.
- **Supplementary point** — a group mean (or extra observation) projected onto an existing map *without* entering the decomposition; used to overlay covariates and spot batch effects.
- **Batch effect / confounding** — nuisance technical variation; confounded when two factors vary together so their effects cannot be separated without a deconfounding batch.
- **Inertia** — weighted sum of squared distances; the multivariate generalization of variance.
- **Mantel coefficient** — correlation between two distance matrices' vectorized entries; significance via permutation (no double centering).
- **RV coefficient** — $[0,1]$ correlation-like similarity between two whole data tables.
- **Canonical Correlation Analysis (CCA)** — linear combinations of two variable sets with maximal mutual correlation; **sCCA** adds an $L_1$ penalty for feature selection when features ≫ samples.
- **Constrained Correspondence Analysis (CCpnA)** — CA forced into the span of explanatory environmental variables; ordination guided by covariates, gentler than full supervision.
- **t-SNE / diffusion map / isomap / LLE** — local, nonlinear embedding methods that prioritize local distances and reveal manifolds/trajectories; t-SNE inter-cluster distances are not metrically interpretable.
