## Why we need this chapter

Closes two gaps left open by the ancestral-reconstruction ingest probe
(`research/experiments/ingest-ancestral-reconstruction/gap-closing.md`, gaps 2b + 2c). This chapter
is the accessible substitute for the paywalled Cunningham 1999 model-comparison point. Pull the
model-selection procedure and the ASR-adequacy cautions precisely — those are the recovery targets.

## Targeted questions

1. **Mk rate structures.** How does the chapter parameterize the Mk model's transition rates —
   equal-rates (ER), symmetric (SYM), and all-rates-different (ARD / "ASY")? How many free rate
   parameters does each carry, and how do they nest?
2. **Model comparison procedure (gap 2c core).** How are competing discrete-evolution models (ER vs
   ARD/ASY) compared? Which statistics/criteria does the chapter use — likelihood-ratio test,
   AIC/AICc/BIC? State the exact decision rule and any numeric threshold (e.g. a ΔAIC cutoff, or
   degrees of freedom for the LRT). **Must-quote** the decision-rule sentence.
3. **Worked example numbers (gap 2c core).** Does the chapter give a worked ER-vs-ASY comparison
   (the squamate / lizard example)? Report the exact model-fit numbers verbatim — log-likelihoods
   and AIC/AICc/BIC values for each model — and the verdict the author draws. Is one model
   decisively preferred, or is the comparison inconclusive? **Must-quote** the author's own
   conclusion about whether the comparison is definitive.
4. **ASR information source (gap 2b).** When reconstructing ancestral states from a fitted Mk model,
   what data does the reconstruction actually use — only the observed (extant tip) states, or more?
   **Must-quote** any statement about what information the reconstruction draws on.
5. **Priors / model dominating the reconstruction (gap 2b).** What does the chapter say about how the
   fitted rates, the model choice, or a prior can dominate or distort an ancestral-state estimate —
   i.e. why a single point reconstruction can be overconfident or inadequate? **Must-quote** the
   caution.
6. **Marginal vs joint / stochastic mapping.** Does the chapter distinguish marginal from joint
   ancestral reconstruction, or introduce stochastic character mapping? What form of uncertainty
   (posterior probabilities, scaled likelihoods, sampled histories) does it recommend reporting
   instead of a single state?
7. **Named failure modes.** Capture every cautionary note: overconfident single-point
   reconstructions, consequences of model misspecification, low information at deep nodes, rate
   estimates unreliable on small trees. For each: the warning sign and the stated consequence.
