## 3. Thesis (from abstract, own words)

As multiple genomes per species become cheap and routine, pan-genome analysis — characterizing
the size of the gene repertoire accessible to a species together with an estimate of how many
whole-genome sequences are needed for proper analysis — is increasingly applied; existing models
vary in accuracy and applicability, and the authors propose a new model of broad applicability
with worked examples.

## 4. Problem & context (from abstract)

- Bacterial genome sequencing became easy/accessible → multiple strains per species are being
  sequenced.
- These multi-strain sets enable in-depth analysis of intra-species diversity.
- Pan-genome analysis characterizes the accessible gene repertoire AND estimates the number of
  whole-genome sequences required for proper analysis.
- "Different models exist for the analysis and their accuracy and applicability depend on the case
  at hand." (abstract, paraphrased — this is the paper's stated motivation for a new model.)
