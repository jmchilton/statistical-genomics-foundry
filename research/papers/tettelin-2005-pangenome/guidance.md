# Guidance — Tettelin 2005 (the pan-genome concept)

Citation: Tettelin H et al. 2005. "Genome analysis of multiple pathogenic isolates of
*Streptococcus agalactiae*: implications for the microbial 'pan-genome'." *PNAS*
102(39):13950–13955. DOI 10.1073/pnas.0506758102. Open URL:
https://www.pnas.org/doi/10.1073/pnas.0506758102 (PMC1216834).

License posture: PNAS 2005, copyright National Academy of Sciences, freely readable →
**own-words summary; verbatim only for functional strings** (defined terms, equation forms,
numeric extrapolations).

Questions (direct attention, do not confirm conclusions):

1. How does the paper DEFINE "pan-genome," "core genome," and "dispensable/character genome"?
   Quote the defining sentences verbatim.
2. Does the paper give a numeric presence threshold for what counts as "core"? What fraction of
   strains? Quote it. (We need to know whether "core = present in 100% of strains" is stated, vs
   the later ≥95%/≥99% conventions.)
3. How does the paper mathematically extrapolate the number of NEW genes per added genome? Capture
   the equation/functional form verbatim (any exponential-decay or power-law expression, and the
   fitted parameter names).
4. Does the paper conclude *S. agalactiae* has an OPEN or CLOSED pan-genome, and on what evidence
   (how many genomes were sequenced)? Quote the openness conclusion.
5. How many genomes did the authors use, and do they state how many MORE would be needed to
   characterize the species? Capture any explicit sampling-adequacy statement.
6. Does the paper anywhere state the phrase "distributed genome" or attribute it? Note if absent.
7. Does the paper apply "Heaps' law" by name? (We suspect the Heaps-law framing is later; note
   whether this 2005 paper uses it at all.)

Silences to record: does the paper define "shell" or "cloud" classes? (We think NOT — those are
later; confirm the 2005 partition is only core vs dispensable.)

Must-extract functional strings: the defining sentences for pan-genome/core/dispensable; the
new-genes extrapolation equation + parameter names; the genome count used; the open/closed verdict.
