## Must capture
- The exact definition of "double dipping" / circular analysis, in the source's own framing.
- The precise CONDITION under which an analysis is distorted: the relationship between the
  selection criterion and the test statistic under the null hypothesis. Does the source say the
  problem is statistical *non-independence* of selection and test (not merely "reusing data")?
  Capture the exact condition — this is the load-bearing distinction.
- The noise/null demonstration: what happens when the procedure is run on data with NO true effect
  (pure noise) — does selection-then-test manufacture apparent structure? Capture the result.
- Any taxonomy/figure classifying selection+test combinations as valid vs. invalid (which
  selection criteria are "safe" because independent of the contrast under the null).
- The recommended remedy: independent data for selection vs. test (split-data / leave-out), and
  the exact caveat about WHEN independence is sufficient (independence must hold under the null).
- Whether the source distinguishes effects on the point estimate (bias) vs. on inference
  (false-positive / error-rate inflation).
